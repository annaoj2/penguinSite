All About Penguins

Penguins are flightless birds that live in the Southern Hemisphere. Although penguins are seabirds, they also spend time on land to rest, to raise their young, and to momentarily escape predators.

Short Penguin Quiz

How many species of penguin are there?

  1. Emperor Penguin


    • Lives in Antarctica
    • Does not build a nest; instead, incubates egg on top of its feet

  2. King Penguin


    • Lives on sub-Antarctic islands, e.g. South Georgia Island
    • Does not build a nest; instead, incubates egg on top of its feet

  3. Adelie Penguin


    • Lives in Antarctica
    • Nests on piles of stones

  4. Chinstrap Penguin


    • Lives on the Antarctic Peninsula
    • Nests on piles of stones

  5. Gentoo Penguin


    • Lives on the Antarctic Peninsula, on sub-Antarctic islands, in the Falklands, and occasionally on islands off the coast of Argentina
    • Nests in mud structures with stones

  6. Southern Rockhopper Penguin


    • Lives on sub-Antarctic islands and in the more temperate Falkland Islands
    • Nests in shallow scrapes lined with vegetation

  7. Humboldt Penguin


    • Lives in Peru
    • Nests in underground burrows

  8. Fiordland Penguin


    • Lives in coastal forests of New Zealand
    • Nests in caves, under vegetation, or in any other hidden natural structure
